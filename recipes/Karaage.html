Description

Very easy to make and my guests enjoy it a lot! It can be served as an appetizer, or over a bed of rice as a quick weekday dinner! I usually marinate the night before to give it more flavor, but if it's a last-minute meal you are preparing, no worries, because there's still dipping sauce! Serve hot with shredded lettuce and a lemon wedge.

Ingredients

  • one ½ tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • one tablespoon honey
  • two teaspoons minced garlic
  • two teaspoons minced fresh ginger
  • one pound skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • one egg
  • one cup panko bread crumbs
  • vegetable oil for frying

Sause

  • ½ cup Japanese mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 3 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • one tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce

Instructions

  • Mix one 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce, one tablespoon honey, garlic, and ginger together in a bowl. Add chicken; turn to coat. Marinate in the refrigerator, eight hours to overnight.

  • Whisk egg in a bowl until smooth.

  • Pour panko bread crumbs into a shallow bowl. Dip chicken pieces in whisked egg and roll in panko until coated.

  • Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Cook chicken in batches in the hot oil until golden brown, about five minutes. Transfer to paper towels to drain excess oil.

  • Mix Japanese mayonnaise, two tablespoons honey, Dijon mustard, mirin, and one tablespoon soy sauce together to make sauce. Pour sauce over chicken.
